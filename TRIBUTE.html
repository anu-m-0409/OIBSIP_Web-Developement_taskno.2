Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 – 2015

Missile Man of India · People's President

“Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.”

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from a humble background to become one of the most respected scientists and leaders in the country.

Dr. Kalam worked extensively with India’s space and defence programs and played a key role in the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. His life was dedicated to science, education, and national development.

Work & Legacy

Known as the “Missile Man of India”, Dr. Kalam was instrumental in the success of the Agni and Prithvi missile projects. He also contributed significantly to India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Beyond science, he inspired millions through his books, lectures, and interactions with students. His vision for a developed India and emphasis on youth empowerment continue to influence future generations.
